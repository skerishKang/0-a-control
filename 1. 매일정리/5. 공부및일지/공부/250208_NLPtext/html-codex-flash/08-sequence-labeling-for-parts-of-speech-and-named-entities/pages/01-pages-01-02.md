- source_text: Dionysius Thrax of Alexandria (c. 100 B.C.), or perhaps someone else (it was a long time ago), wrote a grammatical sketch of Greek (a “technē”) that summarized the linguistic knowledge of his day. This work is the source of an astonishing proportion of modern linguistic vocabulary, including the words syntax, diphthong, clitic, and analogy. Also included are a description of eight parts of speech: noun, verb, pronoun, preposition, adverb, conjunction, participle, and article. Although earlier scholars (including Aristotle as well as the Stoics) had their own lists of parts of speech, it was Thrax’s set of eight that became the basis for descriptions of European languages for the next 2000 years. (All the way to the Schoolhouse Rock educational television shows of our childhood, which had songs about 8 parts of speech, like the late great Bob Dorough’s Conjunction Junction.) The durability of parts of speech through two millennia speaks to their centrality in models of human language.

## Block 9
- type: paragraph
- source_text: Parts of speech (also known as POS) and named entities are useful clues to sentence structure and meaning. Knowing whether a word is a noun or a verb tells us about likely neighboring words (nouns in English are preceded by determiners and adjectives, verbs by nouns) and syntactic structure (verbs have dependency links to nouns), making part-of-speech tagging a key aspect of parsing. Knowing if a named entity like Washington is a name of a person, a place, or a university is important to many natural language processing tasks like question answering, stance detection, or information extraction.
- korean_translation: 품사(POS라고도 함)와 개체명은 문장 구조와 의미를 파악하는 데 유용한 단서가 됩니다. 특정 단어가 명사인지 동사인지 아는 것은 주변에 어떤 단어가 올지(영어에서 명사 앞에는 한정사나 형용사가 오고, 동사 앞에는 명사가 오는 등)와 구문 구조(동사는 명사와 의존 관계를 가짐)에 대한 정보를 제공하므로, 품사 태깅(part-of-speech tagging)은 구문 분석(parsing)의 핵심적인 요소가 됩니다. 또한 'Washington' 같은 개체명이 사람 이름인지, 지명인지, 혹은 대학교 이름인지 판별하는 것은 질의응답, 입장 탐지(stance detection), 정보 추출과 같은 많은 자연어 처리 작업에서 중요합니다.
- study_note: 품사 태깅과 개체명 인식이 자연어 처리의 다운스트림 작업(구문 분석, 질의응답 등)에서 어떤 실질적인 도움을 주는지 설명합니다.

- source_text: Such tasks in which we assign, to each word x_i in an input word sequence, a label y_i, so that the output sequence Y has the same length as the input sequence X are called sequence labeling tasks. ## Block 6
- type: paragraph
- source_text: Parts of speech fall into two broad categories: closed class and open class. Closed classes are those with relatively fixed membership, such as prepositions—new prepositions are rarely coined. By contrast, nouns and verbs are open classes—new nouns and verbs like iPhone or to fax are continually being created or borrowed. Closed class words are generally function words like of, it, and, or you, which tend to be very short, occur frequently, and often have structuring uses in grammar.
- korean_translation: 품사는 크게 두 가지 범주로 나뉩니다. 바로 폐쇄 부류(closed class)와 개방 부류(open class)입니다. 폐쇄 부류는 전치사와 같이 구성원이 비교적 고정된 것들을 말합니다. 새로운 전치사가 만들어지는 일은 거의 없습니다. 반면 명사와 동사는 개방 부류입니다. iPhone이나 'to fax'(팩스를 보내다)처럼 새로운 명사와 동사는 끊임없이 생성되거나 외래어에서 차용됩니다. 폐쇄 부류 단어들은 대개 'of', 'it', 'and', 'you'와 같이 기능어(function words)인 경우가 많으며, 보통 길이가 매우 짧고 빈번하게 등장하며 문법에서 구조적인 역할을 담당하는 경향이 있습니다.

- source_text: Nouns are words for people, places, or things, but include others as well. Many languages, including English, divide common nouns into count nouns and mass nouns.

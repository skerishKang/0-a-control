- source_text: Why would you want to predict upcoming words, or assign probabilities to sentences? Probabilities are essential in any task in which we have to identify words in noisy, ambiguous input, like speech recognition. For a speech recognizer to realize that you said I will be back soonish and not I will be bassoon dish, it helps to know that back soonish is a much more probable sequence than bassoon dish. ## Block 6
- type: paragraph
- source_text: Models that assign probabilities to sequences of words are called language models or LMs. In this chapter we introduce the simplest model that assigns probabilities to sentences and sequences of words, the n-gram. An n-gram is a sequence of n words: a 2-gram (which we'll call bigram) is a two-word sequence of words like "please turn", "turn your", or "your homework", and a 3-gram (a trigram) is a three-word sequence of words like "please turn your", or "turn your homework". We'll see how to use n-gram models to estimate the probability of the last word of an n-gram given the previous words, and also to assign probabilities to entire sequences. In a bit of terminological ambiguity, we usually drop the word "model", and use the term n-gram (and bigram, etc.) to mean either the word sequence itself or the predictive model that assigns it a probability. While n-gram models are much simpler than state-of-the-art neural language models based on the RNNs and transformers we will introduce in Chapter 9, they are an important foundational tool for understanding the fundamental concepts of language modeling.
- korean_translation: 단어 시퀀스에 확률을 할당하는 모델을 언어 모델(language models) 또는 LM이라고 부릅니다. 이 장에서는 문장과 단어 시퀀스에 확률을 부여하는 가장 간단한 형태인 "N-그램(n-gram)" 모델을 소개합니다. N-그램은 $n$개의 단어로 이루어진 시퀀스를 말합니다. 예를 들어 2-그램(보통 바이그램(bigram)이라 부름)은 "please turn", "turn your"와 같은 두 단어의 나열이며, 3-그램(트라이그램(trigram))은 "please turn your"와 같은 세 단어의 나열입니다. 우리는 N-그램 모델을 사용해 이전 단어들이 주어졌을 때 마지막 단어가 나타날 확률을 추론하거나 전체 시퀀스의 확률을 계산하는 방법을 학습할 것입니다. 용어상 약간의 모호함이 있는데, 보통 "모델"이라는 말을 생략하고 "N-그램"이란 용어를 시퀀스 그 자체 혹은 확률을 부여하는 예측 모델 모두를 뜻하는 데 사용하곤 합니다.

## Summary of Extraction Work

I have successfully extracted the complete content of Edwin Lefevre's novel "The Golden Flood" from the HTML file. The novel is divided into three main parts:

### Part One: The Flood
- **Characters**: Mr. Dawson (bank president), George K. Grinnell (mysterious depositor), Williams (assistant cashier)
- **Setting**: Metropolitan National Bank in Wall Street
- **Plot**: A young man named George K. Grinnell begins making enormous gold deposits every Thursday, starting with $100,000 and escalating to millions. Bank president Dawson becomes increasingly concerned about the source of this gold.

### Part Two: The Gold
- **Characters**: William Mellen (richest man in the world), continues with Dawson and Grinnell
- **Plot**: Dawson consults with Mellen about the threat Grinnell poses. They discover Grinnell has a laboratory and appears to be manufacturing gold somehow. The mystery deepens as they realize this could destabilize the entire financial system.

### Part III: The Paradoxical Panic
- **Plot**: The financial implications of unlimited gold production create panic among the wealthy elite. The story explores themes of power, wealth, and the potential for revolutionary change through unlimited resources.
